ORFractal Overview

Outlines of approach and algorithms, formula I use
 
The 3D space is defined as <x, y, z> where x-axis grows left to right, 
y-axis grows down to up, z-axis grows far to near (right-handed system).
The initial nodes are seeded in the space defined by a refernece cube with
W x H x D. Note that nodes could later grow beyond the reference area.
The near-left-down corner of the cube is <-W/2, 0, 0> and
the far-right-up corner of the cube is <W/2, H, -D>. All visual objects
are best positioned within the cube, having x = [-W/2, W/2],
y = [0, H], z = [0, -D].

A scene is collection of nodes that are connected in such a way that
they are generated by fractal growth in a 3D space.
A node is the fundamental building block for a scene.
A node is defined as <P, D, S>, where 
  P = <x, y, z> is used to locate the base position of the node.
  D = <r, a> is the direction, where r is the rotational degree
	from the front toward right around y-axis and a is the angle
	from the top toward left around z-axis.
  S is a set of parameters to define the shape and color.
	It could be width, length, a complex polygon, cylinder,
	and many others depending on render specification.
  Note that <x, y, z>, <r, a> specify a directional relative move from
  the previous position and direction rather than a absolute move.
